Our History

The Egerton University Library was established in 1939 alongside the Egerton Farm School (now Egerton University). Starting as a small collection serving agricultural studies, it has evolved into a modern academic library system spanning multiple campuses.

In 1986, with Egerton's elevation to university status, the library underwent significant expansion. The main library building was completed in 1990, providing expanded facilities for the growing student population.

In 2000, the library expanded to include a new information desk, enhancing the library's services to students and faculty. In 2010, the library expanded to include a new digital repository, enhancing the library's capabilities for research and academic collaboration.

Today, our library system includes specialized collections in agriculture, science, technology, and humanities, serving over 10,000 users annually with both physical and digital resources. We continue to embrace technological advances while maintaining our commitment to academic excellence.

Our mission remains steadfast: to provide accessible, high-quality information services that support teaching, learning, and research at all levels of the university community.
